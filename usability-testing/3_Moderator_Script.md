# Moderator Script

**Project:** Investigating Preventable Infectious Diseases — WHO Immunisation Data Explorer
**Course:** COSC3107 | COSC3108 — Python Programming Studio
**Moderator:** Nguyen Huynh (S4187700)

> Read the **bold quoted** sections aloud to the participant. Plain text are
> instructions for you, the moderator. Keep a relaxed, friendly tone.

---

## Before the participant arrives — setup checklist

- [ ] Application running: `python app.py`, browser open at `http://127.0.0.1:5000/`
- [ ] Browser on the **Home** page, zoom at ~110 %
- [ ] DevTools closed, notifications muted
- [ ] PIF ready (printed or on screen)
- [ ] Note-taking sheet / metrics table ready
- [ ] Timer ready

## 1. Introduction *(approx. 1 minute)*

> **"Hi, thanks for helping me out today. My name is Nguyen, and I'm testing
> a website I built for our Python Programming Studio. It explores World
> Health Organization data about vaccination and infectious diseases.**
>
> **The most important thing to know is this: I'm testing the website, not
> you. There are no right or wrong answers. If something is confusing or
> hard to find, that's not your fault — it's exactly the kind of feedback I
> need to make it better.**
>
> **The whole session will take about 15 to 20 minutes. First a few short
> tasks on the website, then a quick survey at the end."**

Hand over / show the **Participant Information Form**.

> **"Before we start, please take a moment to read this information form. It
> explains the purpose, that your participation is completely voluntary, and
> that your responses stay anonymous. You can stop at any time, for any
> reason. Do you have any questions about it?"**

Confirm consent (verbal is fine, tick the box on the form).

> **"Is it okay if I take some written notes while you work? I won't record
> any audio or video."**

Wait for agreement.

## 2. Think-aloud instructions *(approx. 30 seconds)*

> **"One technique I'd like you to use: please think aloud as you go. That
> means say out loud what you're looking at, what you're trying to do, and
> what you expect to happen when you click something. If you get stuck or
> confused, just say so — don't worry about being polite about it.**
>
> **I won't be able to help you much during the tasks, because I want to see
> how the site works on its own. But if you're truly stuck, let me know and
> we'll move on. Ready?"**

Start the session. Note the start time.

---

## 3. Tasks

> For each task: read the scenario aloud, start the timer, observe silently,
> and record metrics. Do **not** point at the screen or suggest where to
> click. If the participant asks "is this right?", respond neutrally:
> **"Whatever makes sense to you — there's no wrong answer."**

### Task 1 — Big Picture *(Level 1A)*

> **"You've just opened this site for the first time. Take a look at the home
> page, and tell me out loud — what is this site about, and what kind of data
> does it cover?"**

Observe: do they read the fact cards? The disease list? Record facts
identified and time.

### Task 2 — Mission & author *(Level 1B)*

> **"Now, imagine you want to know who this website is designed for, and who
> built it. Go ahead and find that out."**

Observe: do they find the Mission link? Do they spot the personas and the
author? Record navigation errors.

### Task 3 — Filter coverage *(Level 2A)*

> **"Let's say you want to see which countries reached at least 90 percent
> coverage for the MCV1 vaccine in the year 2020. Show me how you'd do
> that."**

Observe: do they find the filters and the Apply action? Do they understand
Table 1? Record filter mistakes and time.

### Task 4 — Compare regions by sort *(Level 2A)*

> **"Now you want to see, for each region, how many of its countries reached
> that 90 percent benchmark — and you want the regions ordered from the most
> to the fewest. Show me."**

Observe: do they read Table 2? Do they discover the sort control unaided?

### Task 5 — High-burden low-income countries *(Level 2B)*

> **"Switch gears — imagine you're planning a measles vaccination campaign.
> Find the measles cases per 100,000 people for low-income countries in 2022,
> and tell me which country was the worst affected."**

Observe: do they reach the Infections × Economy page? Do they set all three
filters correctly? Do they understand "per 100,000"? Do they sort?

### Task 6 — Cross-phase summary *(Level 2B)*

> **"Staying on that same page — can you tell me which economic phase had the
> most total measles cases in 2022?"**

Observe: do they notice the summary table below the detail table?

---

## 4. Wrap-up *(approx. 1 minute)*

Stop the timer.

> **"That's all the tasks — thank you, that was really helpful. Before we
> finish, I have a few quick questions about your overall experience."**

Hand over the **Survey** (or open it on screen).

While they complete it, you may ask 1–2 open follow-ups verbally if time
allows:

> **"Was there any moment where you felt unsure about what to do next?"**
>
> **"If you could change one thing about the site, what would it be?"**

## 5. Closing

> **"That's everything. Thanks so much for your time and your honest feedback
> — it genuinely helps me improve the design. Enjoy the rest of your day!"**

Record the participant code, total session time, and file the notes.

---

## Moderator do's and don'ts

| Do | Don't |
| -- | ----- |
| Stay neutral and encouraging | Lead the participant or point at the screen |
| Let silences happen — give them time | Fill every pause or rescue them too early |
| Record exact quotes | Paraphrase away the participant's confusion |
| Remind them to think aloud if they go quiet | React with "yes!" or "no" to their actions |
| Thank them genuinely | Defend the design when they criticise it |
