## 1. Invitation

You are invited to take part in a usability test of a web application built
for the Python Programming Studio. Before you decide whether to participate,
please read the information below. You may ask the moderator any questions at
any time.

## 2. Purpose of the study

The purpose of this study is to evaluate how easy and intuitive the web
application is to use. We are testing the **website, not you**. There are no
right or wrong answers, and any difficulty you experience is valuable
feedback that helps us improve the design.

The application lets users explore World Health Organization immunisation
data — vaccination coverage, infection rates, and how these vary by country,
region, year, and economic status.

## 3. What participation involves

If you agree to take part, you will be asked to:

1. Complete a short set of tasks using the web application (approximately
   **10–15 minutes**).
2. Think aloud as you work, describing what you are looking for and what you
   expect to happen.
3. Answer a short survey about your experience after the tasks
   (approximately **3–5 minutes**).

The moderator may observe your actions and take written notes. No audio,
video, or screen recording will be captured unless you give separate verbal
consent at the start of the session.

## 4. Voluntary participation

Your participation is entirely **voluntary**. You may:

- Decline to answer any question.
- Skip any task you do not wish to complete.
- Stop and withdraw from the session at any time, for any reason, without
  penalty or need to explain.

Withdrawing will have no effect on your standing in the course.

## 5. Confidentiality and data handling

- No personally identifying information (name, student number, email) will
  be recorded in the test results.
- Each participant is referred to only by an anonymous code (e.g.
  "Participant 1").
- Notes and survey responses are used solely for the purpose of improving
  this application and completing the associated assessment.
- Data is stored only for the duration of the course assessment and is not
  shared outside the project team and the teaching staff.

## 6. Risks and benefits

There are no anticipated risks beyond those of ordinary computer use. There
is no direct benefit to you, but your feedback directly improves the design
of the application and contributes to the learning outcomes of the course.

contact the researcher at **S4187700@rmit.edu.vn**.
